# Line Up Pilot Launch Checklist

Use this checklist before giving a restaurant its pilot login. The goal is to prove one complete training loop with real managers and staff before expanding the library.

## 1. Prepare The Workspace

- Confirm the restaurant name, address, website, and logo in Workspace Settings.
- Confirm the Account Owner can log in and reach the Workspace Dashboard.
- Decide who will be the restaurant's day-to-day Line Up manager.
- Write down the pilot start date and the date for the first feedback meeting.

## 2. Build A Small Real Library

- Create three to five Training Categories that match the restaurant's language.
- Add at least five real Training Pages.
- Include at least one food item, one beverage item, and one SOP or service standard.
- Keep new pages as drafts until the restaurant manager reviews them.
- Publish only pages that are ready for staff.

## 3. Make The Material Testable

- Add Testable Staff Knowledge to every pilot Training Page.
- Create one short quiz with five to ten questions.
- Review generated questions and wrong answers before publishing.
- Confirm the passing score with the restaurant manager.

## 4. Test The Complete Staff Flow

- Invite one manager and one staff member.
- Open each invitation and confirm the correct role is assigned.
- Confirm staff cannot open manager pages.
- Confirm staff can see only published Training Pages.
- Have the staff member take the quiz.
- Confirm the staff score appears in My Progress.
- Confirm the same result appears in Staff Progress for the manager.

## 5. Check The Restaurant Boundary

- Use a second test restaurant workspace.
- Confirm neither restaurant can see the other's pages, team, quizzes, results, files, or settings.
- Confirm a disabled staff member cannot access the workspace.

## 6. Launch The Pilot

- Choose a small staff group for week one.
- Give managers one clear place to report mistakes or confusing wording.
- Schedule a 20-minute feedback check after the first three shifts.
- Track content corrections, invite problems, quiz confusion, and manager requests.
- Avoid adding every document at once; prove the daily workflow first.

## Pilot Success Signals

- Managers can add or update a Training Page without developer help.
- Staff can join from an invitation without getting stuck.
- Staff can find the right information before service.
- Quiz results save correctly and managers can understand the progress page.
- The restaurant asks to add more staff or more training material.

## Before Charging A Customer

- Complete backend-enforced tenant authorization for sensitive data operations.
- Add production monitoring and error reporting.
- Confirm Stripe Checkout, webhooks, and billing status updates in production.
- Verify email sending from an approved Line Up domain.
- Document customer support, data correction, and account cancellation procedures.
